# Methodology

## Why We Built This As Skills

Most hook advice online collapses three different jobs into one messy blob:

- inspiration
- wording
- actual opening mechanics

That is why teams often collect hundreds of examples and still produce weak openings.

They are collecting references, but not extracting reusable logic.

This repo tries to fix that.

## Distillation Model

Our working model is simple:

1. collect many short-form opening examples
2. separate strong openings from attractive but weak ones
3. ask what concrete viewer question each opening creates
4. inspect when the product appears
5. inspect what visible evidence makes the opening believable
6. convert repeatable patterns into reusable skill instructions

## Input Pool

The broader hook system behind this repo has been informed by analysis across **1,000+ short-form hooks, benchmark openings, reference clips, and hook-adjacent prompt examples**.

That pool includes:

- UGC-style ad openings
- creator-led product intros
- pain-first openings
- proof-led openings
- curiosity-led openings
- visual interruption patterns
- benchmark clips and contact sheets
- rough internal prompt drafts that failed

This does **not** mean every example was equally useful.

Many examples were useful precisely because they showed failure:

- product too early
- question too vague
- proof too fake
- frame too clean and ad-like
- viewer curiosity not earned

## What We Actually Extract

We do not treat hooks as swipe-file templates.

We extract reusable operating objects:

- `viewerQuestion`
- `hookMechanism`
- `productRevealRule`
- `visibleEvidence`
- `mustCopyVisualGrammar`
- `forbiddenDrift`

Those objects are more reusable than literal lines.

## Why The Repo Is Split Into 5 Skills

Hook work becomes more reliable when each stage has a clear job:

### 1. `pattern-router`

Decides the opening route and the segment job.

### 2. `hook-design`

Locks the hook mechanism and product timing into a stable brief.

### 3. `reference-decode`

Turns benchmark material into reusable structure.

### 4. `visual-hook`

Translates opening logic into frame-one or first-3-second visual strength.

### 5. `prompt-preflight-qa`

Catches drift before generation.

## What We Optimize For

We optimize for:

- stronger stop-scroll logic
- clearer viewer questions
- lower ad-detection risk
- better downstream prompt controllability

We do **not** optimize for:

- sounding flashy
- collecting generic "viral hook" templates
- pretending every niche should use the same opening style

## What This Repo Does Not Claim

This repo does not guarantee:

- higher watch-through rate
- better hook rate
- cheaper CPA
- broader business success

It is an operational layer, not a guarantee machine.

Its job is to help teams make better opening decisions, faster and more consistently.
